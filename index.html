Work

I work for the W3C Brazil Office, hosted by the Steering Internet Committee of Brazil (CGI.br). I'm responsible for researching issues around Web technologies that can change perspectives and and kickstart initiatives that foster a more open, democratic and free Internet.

Previously, I worked for ONU at UNDP as technical consultant for innovation processes in democracy. Within this general I had the opportunity to work at the Presidency of Brazil, where I did research in design and software, and produced software mockups aiming at the creation of better digital tools for citizen participation. Also at the United Nations, I worked with public consultation for law-making processes, regarding the Marco Civil da Internet and other related bills and laws such as Online Privacy. I don't especially love democracy, but I do believe that it's the best bet we have for now.

As a Designer, I made a bunch of interesting things. Two of them won the the biggest human rights prize in Brazil - the Vladimir Herzog Award. I also worked with data visualization, infographics, 3D representation, jewelry & furniture design. (I'm passionate about the properties of materials.)

I also had the honour of being a teacher at University of Brasilia, at Industrial Design program. To see more, go to my resume to see the proper listing.

Epitome

My life happened immersed in extreme and unusual experiences. I grew up travelling and spent my youth divided between computers and nature, surrounded by the unique Cerrado bioma, in Brasilia, the capital of Brasil.

My father, an engineer, put me on the path towards computer programming when I was 12 teaching me some Lisp. He also taught me that things like love for freedom and the desire for subversion are within the heart, even if you are in a military garb.

That's why I graduated in Industrial Design: a bit of synesthesia, curiosity and the need to reproduce mixed impressions gathered in different experiences in tangible interfaces, from furniture and industrial machines to websites - this took me to where I am now.

I strongly believe that technology exists for change, but only if it is continuus. The way we interact with each other and with machines will shape the future.

This is a world that needs to be more equal and responsible (and maybe even more fun!) - tech isn't a miracle, it's part of our system.

We have to take control of this system.
