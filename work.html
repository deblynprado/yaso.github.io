Working to W3C Brazil Office, which is hosted by the Steering Internet Committee of Brazil, I'm responsible for research Web technologies that can change views and start actions toward a more open, democratic and free Internet.

In my previous job I worked for UNESCO as a consultant for innovation processes in democracy, at the United Nations Development Programme project. In this position I could contribute at the Presidency of Brazil, where I produced research, design and software mockup to show paths to achieve better digital tools for citizen participation. Also with the United Nations, I worked with public consultation, in law-making processes, for the Marco Civil da Internet, Data & Privacy, Digital Rights and others, at the Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Culture and so on so forth. I don't especially like democracy, but I do believe that it's the best bet we have for change everything.

In my early days of official work I made a bunch of interesting things at journalism and datavis. Two of them won the biggest human rights prize of Brazil - the Vladimir Herzog Award (at 2009 and 2008). I also worked with dataViz, Infographic, jewels (yes, that!) and furniture.
