2014/2013

Currently working for The Brazilian Internet Steering Committee CGI.br, represented by NIC.br, at W3C Brazil Office, as technical leader. My role is to apply standards in software, lead innovation processes and, research Web's relevant aspects that can affect Brazil's technological scenario. I also try to make software, and other tangible products that experiment new technologies.

I'm co-chair at Data on the Web Best Practices Working Group and

Member of the audit comitee of the Open Knowledge Brazil Chapter, known as OKBr.

To see details, go to resumeor projects

2012/13

Worked for W3C Brazil Office as Developer Relations and Web Specialist. In this role, my work was to find the best practices to make the community development and build it aroud Open Data and Open Web. Thus, I conceived Hackathons, contests, meetings and workshops between communities of different circles. I organized Desarrolando America Latina, Brazil chapter, in 2012 and 2013, coordinated the OD4D challenge and concieved and executed hackatons around Open Data and Open Web. I also developed the interface of some sites and coordinate coverage on social networks for two conferences that were organized by the office: the www2013 and the Webbr2013. This caused a shift on the way that Web Interfaces are conceived at the W3C Brazil Office. It also added social participation on conferences as a prerequisite for press coverage.

2011

Worked at in special projects that aim to use technology to raise citizen participation in democratic processes. To view detailed cases, go to Projects

I volunteered for the Open Data portal of the Federal Government of Brazil and worked with the Ministry of Planning team at the National Infraestructure for Open Data , connected with the Open Government Partnership initiative.

2010/11

Before that, I worked for the United Nations Development Programme as technical consultant in innovation processes for democracy at the Ministry of Justice. My role was develop solutions for bills proposed by the Secretariat of Institutional Affairs, including public consultation and new processes for democracy mediated by software. I end up producing 4 public consultationsand guidelines that gave rise to the participation portal of the Secretariat.

2010

Worked for the Ministry of Culture, as part of the technical staff of "Cultura Digital", developing open projects for decentralization and distribution of content produced whithin the governamental context. In this circumstances we produced prototypes like Cervo and Videre, both projects of public libraries distributed in federated servers using xmpp as a protocol to distribute media. Also, the team made the public consultation for the Brazilian Civil Rights Framework for the Internet and for the Copyright Law reform.

2009

As technical coordinator of a developer team at Brazil Communication Company [EBC], I spent the year working with alternative protocols to distribute and federate multimedia content. We used a daily journal from TV to generate a prototype of a platform called Jarvis. It turned out to be a seed that was later watered in the Digital Culture Secreatariat of the Ministry of Culture.

Ocasionally we made some websites and I worked with journalists in a multimedia story that won the Vladimir Herzog prize for Amnesty and Human Rights in it's 31 edition, in the Internet category.

2008

As UX Designer and Web developer I worked with multimedia content and was part of the team that won, for the 1st time at a public news agency, the most important prize in journalism and human rights in Brasil, the Vladimir Herzog Prize prize for Amnesty and Human Rights, with an multimedia story about the "Quilombolas". My role was to design and develop the platform and infographics for these two pieces. I edited some video too, just for *fun*.

2007

As user experience, user interface designer and Flash Developer (who didn't code ActionScript before HTML5? :-D) I worked with datavis and infographic pieces for the Agência Brazil, which was the public news agency of Radiobras (an equivalent to BBC) .

In this role I worked with non linear narratives, digital storytelling, hipervideo experiences, data visualization and infographics. Some projects are at the projects page. Others were lost, because they offended the government with contrary positions, such as the multimedia storytelling about the power plants on the Madeira River.

2006

My 1st job for the government, working for the public news agency of Brazil, called Radiobras, as Interface Designer and FrontEnd for the new online news agency. We made it using Open Source Software and Creative Commons Licences for the 1st time in Brazil. To see the project detailed, go to projects page.

In parallel, I worked for small companies of Internet infrastrucure sector as a consultant on Digital Marketing strategy. At Redecomm we conducted the company's strategic planning, using methodologies like Domain Drive Design. The company won a prize entrepreneurship given by University of Brasilia in 2006.

Before 2006 I was AutoCad freelancer and worked for independent engineers in Brazil and Europe. My favorite ones were civil engineers specialized in metal structures, boats and bridges. Also worked with AutoCad for the jewerly artisan and artist Clementina Duarte, and for my own father, a great engineer". <3.
