:~$Work

    2014

    Working for The Brazilian Internet Steering Committee (CGI.br), represented by NIC.br, at W3C Brazil Office, as technical leader. My role is to apply standards in software, lead innovation processes and, finally, I research Web's relevant aspects that can affect Brazil's technological scenario.

    2011

    Before that, I worked for the United Nations Development Programme as technical consultant in innovation processes for democracy. In this role, I worked at the Ministry of Justice and at the Presidency of Brazil in special projects that aim to use technology to raise citizen participation in democratic processes.

    I also volunteered in 2011 for the Open Data portal of the Federal Government of Brazil and worked with the Ministry of Planning at the infra needed to do the job.

    2010

    I also worked at the Ministry of Culture, as part of the technical staff of "Cultura Digital", developing open projects for decentralization of content produced to be public. At the Cultura Digital secretary I was part of the team that made the public consultation for the Brazilian Civil Rights Framework for the Internet and for the Copyright reform bill in 2010.

    2009

    Before that, I coordinated a developer team at Brazil Communication Company [EBC], working with alternative protocols to distribute and federate multimedia content, in a project called "Jarvis".

    2008

    In 2008 I was part of the team that won the most important prize in journalism and human rights in Brasil, the Vladimir Herzog Prize, with an multimedia story about illiteracy. In 2007 we won the same prize with another Multimedia storytelling piece about "Quilombolas". My role was to design and develop the platform and infographics for these two pieces.

    2007

    At that time, I was UX/UI designer and Flash Developer (who didn't code ActionScript before HTML5? :-D) and worked under coordination of "Multimedia Executive Editor"

    2006

    I worked also for the public news agency of Brazil, as Interface Designer and FrontEnd for the new online agency. We made it using Open Source Software and Creative Commons Licences for the 1st time in Brazil.
