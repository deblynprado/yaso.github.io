Work

Working to W3C Brazil Office, which is hosted by the Steering Internet Committee of Brazil, I'm responsible for research and practice issues around the Web technology that can change views and start actions toward a more open, democratic and free Internet.

Before I worked for UNESCO as a consultant for innovation processes in democracy. At the presidency, I produced research, design and software mockup to point paths to achieve better digital tools for citizen participation. Also with the United Nations, I worked with public consultation, in law-making processes, for the Marco Civil da Internet, Data Privacy among others. I don't especially like democracy, but I do believe that it's the best bet we have for change everything.

And, as a Designer, I made a bunch of beautiful things. Two of them won the biggest human rights prize of Brazil - the Vladimir Herzog Award. Worked with dataViz, Infographic and jewels (yes, that!) and furniture. I'm passionate by the property of materials.

Etipome

My life happened immersed in extreme and unusual experiences. Grow up travelling, spent my youth splitting my time between the nature at the heart of Cerrado and computers in Brasilia, capital of Brazil.

That's why I graduated in Industrial Design: a bit of synesthesia, curiosity and the need to reproduce mixed impressions gathered in different experiences in tangible interfaces, from furniture and industrial machines to websites - this took me to where I am now.

I strongly believe that technology exists for change, but only if it is continuum. The way we interact with each other and with machines is decisive to our future.

This is a world that needs to be more equal and responsible (and maybe fun!) - tech isn't a miracle, it's part of our system. We have to take control of this system.
